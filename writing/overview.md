I recently wrapped up a personal project called "making-an-outdoor-living-room," and let me tell you, it was a journey. This repo is all about crafting a space in my backyard that feels like an extension of my home. 

## What Does the Repo Do?

At its core, this repository houses a detailed plan for transforming outdoor spaces into cozy, functional living rooms. It includes:

- **Design ideas:** Mood boards, layout options, and furniture suggestions.
- **Material recommendations:** Choosing weather-resistant options that don’t sacrifice style.
- **DIY guides:** Step-by-step instructions for building furniture and surrounding structures.

You can find the project [here](https://github.com/justin-napolitano/making-an-outdoor-living-room).

## Why Did I Build It?

With the pandemic pushing us all to spend more time at home, I realized the importance of having an inviting outdoor area. I wanted a place to relax, entertain friends, and just enjoy nature without straying too far from my living room comfort. 

An outdoor living room was the perfect solution. It combined the tranquility of the outdoors with the functionality and warmth of an indoor space. I couldn't find a one-size-fits-all solution in the market, so I decided to take matters into my own hands.

## Key Design Decisions

The design of this project was critical to its success. Here are some key decisions I made:

- **Zoning:** I divided the space into sections; lounging, dining, and a fire pit area. Each section has its own vibe and purpose.
- **Furniture:** I opted for modular furniture to allow flexibility. You can rearrange or swap pieces based on the occasion.
- **Materials:** I prioritized weather-resistant and durable options like teak and resin wicker, ensuring longevity against the elements.
  
These decisions were not made lightly. I wanted something that could withstand New England winters but still be visually appealing.

## Stack & Tools

Building this outdoor living room involved both physical tools and digital resources:

- **Digital Design Tools:** I utilized SketchUp for 3D modeling to visualize the space.
- **Physical Tools:** Standard carpentry tools and power tools for building furniture.
- **Sustainability Checklist:** I created a list of sustainable materials that are both eco-friendly and durable.

This combination of tools made the process engaging. Plus, it felt satisfying to create a plan that was both practical and aspirational.

## Tradeoffs

Like any project, there were tradeoffs along the way:

- **Cost vs. Quality:** I could've easily gone the budget route, but I opted for quality materials. I believe it's worth investing in durability, especially for outdoor settings.
- **Time vs. Simplicity:** Some designs I considered were much simpler but wouldn’t provide the same aesthetic. I chose complexity to create a unique experience.

These decisions made the project more challenging but ultimately more rewarding. It’s important to balance aspiration with feasibility.

## Future Improvements
